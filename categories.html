Residential

Single-Family Homes

Single-family homes are the most common property type we purchase. Whether you have a starter home, a mid-range family home, or a luxury estate, we buy them all for cash in any condition.

Common situations we see with residential sellers include inherited homes that need work, properties with deferred maintenance, homes going through foreclosure, divorce situations, and simply homeowners who want to skip the traditional listing process.

Condition does not matter. We buy homes with roof damage, foundation issues, mold, fire damage, water damage, pest damage, code violations, outdated systems, and cosmetic wear. No property is too far gone.

  • Single-family detached homes
  • Townhouses and row homes
  • Patio homes and villas
  • Luxury homes and estates
  • Fixer-uppers and tear-downs

Condominiums

Condos and Townhouses

Selling a condo can be complicated by HOA restrictions, special assessments, pending litigation, and association approval requirements. Cash buyers bypass many of these obstacles because there is no mortgage lender requiring association certification.

We buy condos even when the HOA has pending lawsuits, the building needs structural repairs (a growing issue in Florida after the Surfside tragedy and subsequent inspection mandates), special assessments have been levied, or the condo is in a non-warrantable building that traditional lenders will not finance.

  • High-rise and mid-rise condos
  • Garden-style condominiums
  • Townhouse-style condos
  • Non-warrantable condos
  • Condos with HOA issues or assessments

Multi-Family

Duplexes, Triplexes, and Apartment Buildings

Multi-family properties present unique selling challenges: existing tenants, lease agreements, rent rolls, maintenance backlogs, and property management headaches. Cash buyers simplify the process by purchasing multi-family properties with tenants in place - no evictions required.

Whether you own a duplex, a small apartment building, or a larger complex, we analyze the property based on current rents, occupancy, condition, and location. Tired landlords are one of our most common seller types.

  • Duplexes and triplexes
  • Fourplexes (4 units)
  • Small apartment buildings (5-20 units)
  • Large apartment complexes (20+ units)
  • Properties with problem tenants

Vacant Land

Land, Lots, and Acreage

Vacant land is one of the hardest property types to sell traditionally because most agents do not specialize in land, and mortgage lenders rarely finance raw land purchases. Cash is often the only practical way to sell land quickly.

We purchase residential building lots, commercial parcels, agricultural acreage, waterfront land, wooded tracts, and landlocked parcels. Whether you inherited land, bought it as an investment that did not pan out, or simply want to stop paying property taxes on unused land, we can help.

  • Residential building lots
  • Commercial and industrial parcels
  • Agricultural acreage
  • Waterfront and lakefront land
  • Wooded and undeveloped tracts
  • Landlocked or difficult-access parcels
