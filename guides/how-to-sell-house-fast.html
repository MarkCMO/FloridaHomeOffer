How Fast Can You Sell in Florida?

Timeline by method: Cash sale: 7-14 days. Auction: 30-45 days. Agent (priced right): 60-90 days. Agent (overpriced): 120-180+ days. FSBO: 90-180+ days. The fastest option is always a cash sale because it eliminates financing, appraisals, inspections, and contingencies entirely.

How Does Selling for Cash Work?

Day 1: submit property details. Day 1-2: receive written cash offer. Day 2: accept or decline (no obligation). Day 3-5: title company conducts title search. Day 7-14: close at title company, receive funds via wire. No showings, staging, repairs, open houses, appraisals, or financing contingencies.

Best Pricing Strategy for Speed?

Price aggressively from day one. Properties at 95-97% of comparable sales generate 3-5x more showings in the first two weeks. After 30 days on market, buyer interest drops significantly and you end up reducing below where you should have started. Overpricing is the #1 cause of slow sales.

Does Condition Affect Speed?

Move-in ready homes sell 40-60% faster. But renovation time (4-12 weeks + $10K-$100K) often exceeds the benefit. For properties needing significant work, selling as-is to a cash buyer is the fastest path by far.

What Situations Need the Fastest Sale?

Pre-foreclosure (limited time before auction), divorce (both parties need to move on), job relocation, inherited property (paying taxes on unused property), health emergencies, and double mortgage situations. Cash buyers specialize in all of these time-critical scenarios.
