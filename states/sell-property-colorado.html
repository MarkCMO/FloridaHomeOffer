Colorado Real Estate Laws Sellers Should Know

Colorado is a title state. Sellers must provide a Seller's Property Disclosure form. Transfer tax is $0.01 per $100 of sale price at the state level, with additional local taxes. Colorado uses a deed of trust, and foreclosures can be either judicial or non-judicial (public trustee sale is most common, taking about 110-125 days).

Understanding these legal differences is important when selling property in Colorado. A cash sale simplifies the process because there are no lender requirements, no appraisal contingencies, and the closing timeline is driven by the title search and document preparation - not bank approvals.
