Ohio Real Estate Laws Sellers Should Know

Ohio is a title state with title companies handling closings, though attorneys are commonly involved. Sellers must provide a Residential Property Disclosure Form. Transfer tax is $1 per $1,000 at the state level plus county permissive taxes. Ohio does not use a deed of trust - it uses traditional mortgages. Foreclosures are judicial, typically taking 6-12 months.

Understanding these legal differences is important when selling property in Ohio. A cash sale simplifies the process because there are no lender requirements, no appraisal contingencies, and the closing timeline is driven by the title search and document preparation - not bank approvals.
