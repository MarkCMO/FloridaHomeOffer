Illinois Real Estate Laws Sellers Should Know

Illinois has dual closing options - attorneys or title companies can handle closings, but attorney involvement is customary in Chicagoland. Sellers must provide a Residential Real Property Disclosure Report. Transfer tax is $0.50 per $500, plus additional Chicago ($7.50 per $500) and Cook County ($0.50 per $500) taxes. Foreclosures are judicial and can take 12-18 months.

Understanding these legal differences is important when selling property in Illinois. A cash sale simplifies the process because there are no lender requirements, no appraisal contingencies, and the closing timeline is driven by the title search and document preparation - not bank approvals.
