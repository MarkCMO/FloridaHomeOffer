New Hampshire Real Estate Laws Sellers Should Know

New Hampshire is an attorney state - lawyers handle closings. Sellers must provide a Disclosure of Information on Lead-Based Paint for pre-1978 homes, but there is no state-mandated property disclosure form. Transfer tax is $7.50 per $1,000 of sale price, split between buyer and seller. There is no state income tax or sales tax. Foreclosures can be judicial or non-judicial.

Understanding these legal differences is important when selling property in New Hampshire. A cash sale simplifies the process because there are no lender requirements, no appraisal contingencies, and the closing timeline is driven by the title search and document preparation - not bank approvals.
