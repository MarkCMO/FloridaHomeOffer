Georgia Real Estate Laws Sellers Should Know

Georgia is an attorney state - a licensed attorney must supervise the closing. Sellers must provide a Seller's Property Disclosure Statement. Transfer tax is $1.00 per $1,000 of sale price. Georgia uses a security deed rather than a mortgage, and foreclosures are non-judicial, typically completed in 30-60 days.

Understanding these legal differences is important when selling property in Georgia. A cash sale simplifies the process because there are no lender requirements, no appraisal contingencies, and the closing timeline is driven by the title search and document preparation - not bank approvals.
