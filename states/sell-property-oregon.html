Oregon Real Estate Laws Sellers Should Know

Oregon is an escrow state with escrow companies handling closings. Sellers must provide a Seller's Property Disclosure Statement. There is no state transfer tax, though some counties have their own. Oregon has no sales tax. Foreclosures are typically non-judicial through a trust deed process, taking about 180 days.

Understanding these legal differences is important when selling property in Oregon. A cash sale simplifies the process because there are no lender requirements, no appraisal contingencies, and the closing timeline is driven by the title search and document preparation - not bank approvals.
