Massachusetts Real Estate Laws Sellers Should Know

Massachusetts is an attorney state - lawyers must be involved in closings. Sellers must provide a Property Transfer Certification. Massachusetts uses a unique excise tax system at $2.28 per $500 of sale price. Some properties use the Torrens title registration system. Foreclosures can be non-judicial if the mortgage includes a power of sale clause.

Understanding these legal differences is important when selling property in Massachusetts. A cash sale simplifies the process because there are no lender requirements, no appraisal contingencies, and the closing timeline is driven by the title search and document preparation - not bank approvals.
