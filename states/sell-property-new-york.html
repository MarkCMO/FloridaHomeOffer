New York Real Estate Laws Sellers Should Know

New York is an attorney state - lawyers are required at closings. Sellers in NYC must provide extensive disclosures or pay a $500 credit. Transfer tax is $2 per $500 at the state level, plus NYC has its own transfer tax. The mansion tax adds 1-3.9% on properties over $1 million. Foreclosures are judicial and can take 12-36 months in NYC.

Understanding these legal differences is important when selling property in New York. A cash sale simplifies the process because there are no lender requirements, no appraisal contingencies, and the closing timeline is driven by the title search and document preparation - not bank approvals.
