Wisconsin Real Estate Laws Sellers Should Know

Wisconsin has a unique system where both title companies and attorneys can handle closings, with no requirement for attorney involvement. Sellers must provide a Real Estate Condition Report. Transfer tax is $3 per $1,000 of sale price. Foreclosures are judicial, typically taking 6-12 months.

Understanding these legal differences is important when selling property in Wisconsin. A cash sale simplifies the process because there are no lender requirements, no appraisal contingencies, and the closing timeline is driven by the title search and document preparation - not bank approvals.
