Rhode Island Real Estate Laws Sellers Should Know

Rhode Island is an attorney state - lawyers must handle closings. Sellers must provide a Real Estate Condition Report. Transfer tax is $2.30 per $500 of sale price. Rhode Island foreclosures can be either judicial or non-judicial through a statutory power of sale, typically taking 2-6 months.

Understanding these legal differences is important when selling property in Rhode Island. A cash sale simplifies the process because there are no lender requirements, no appraisal contingencies, and the closing timeline is driven by the title search and document preparation - not bank approvals.
