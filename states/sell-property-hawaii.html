Hawaii Real Estate Laws Sellers Should Know

Hawaii is an escrow state where escrow companies handle closings. Sellers must provide a Seller's Real Property Disclosure Statement. Conveyance tax ranges from $0.15 to $1.25 per $100 depending on sale price. Hawaii has a unique HARPTA withholding tax of 7.25% on out-of-state sellers.

Understanding these legal differences is important when selling property in Hawaii. A cash sale simplifies the process because there are no lender requirements, no appraisal contingencies, and the closing timeline is driven by the title search and document preparation - not bank approvals.
